The Flamingo!

As you head toward the river, you spot a Flamingo! It is standing in the water with a group of Flamingos. Flamingos form the largest flocks among birds, and can gather with more than one million others. The Flamingo has good hearing, but little to no sense of smell. Once the Flamingo sheds a feather, it quickly loses its bright color. This bird eats small crustaceans and algae. It usually lays only one egg at a time.

When you find the Flamingo, you join it in the water and stand on one leg to fit in. You enjoy a nice moment among the flock of birds.

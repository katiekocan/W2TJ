The Jaguar!

In the brush of the jungle, you find a Jaguar! The Jaguar is the third largest big cat, only smaller than lions and tigers. It likes to stick around a body of water, like a river or a lake, and may go “fishing” by waving its tail over the water to attract fish that it then catches and eats. Every Jaguar has a unique spot pattern—no two are the same! This cat can reach all levels of the jungle, as it can also climb trees and swim in the river.

You stop where you are once you see the Jaguar. You do not want to disturb it, so you stay still until it falls asleep in a grassy patch underneath a tree. You realize you are also tired and head back to your camp to take a nap.
